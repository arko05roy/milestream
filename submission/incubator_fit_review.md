# Incubator Fit Review: MileStream

## Verdict

Strong fit

## Why it fits HACD

- The core problem — fake freelancer identities and unverifiable credentials — maps directly to HACD's formation-cost and per-lot identity model.
- Supply is hard-capped at 200 HACD lots, matching a reviewable founding contractor cohort.
- Hybrid structure is justified: MSTR (FT) carries fungible credential weight while the HACD name (NFT side) is the unique Contractor ID.
- Formation cost in HAC creates a visible, on-chain cost basis reference that distinguishes MileStream contractors from zero-cost profile spam.
- Stack removal burns MSTR and retires the Contractor ID, keeping supply linked to active credentials.

## What HACD adds

- **Anti-sybil credentialing**: Creating 200 fake contractor profiles would require 200 HACD units and 10,000 HAC in formation costs.
- **Portable identity**: Contractor ID is recorded on Hacash, not locked in a platform database.
- **PoW provenance**: Each credential is tied to a mined HACD container.
- **Scarcity signal**: 200 founding slots communicates a reviewable cohort to clients.

## GrowStreams alignment

- MileStream's payment layer is designed to use GrowStreams for milestone streaming — aligned with the quest sponsor at https://www.growstreams.xyz/app/projects/HACD.
- HACD handles trust and identity; GrowStreams handles payment flow. Clear separation.

## Main concerns

- Payment streaming is a product integration, not enforced by the Stack protocol. Copy must not imply the token holds escrow funds.
- MileStream is not a licensed escrow service or dispute arbiter.
- Hybrid complexity: copy must separate fungible MSTR from unique Contractor ID.
- First-phase 20 lots require defined pilot contractor eligibility.
- Contractor directory and GrowStreams pilots are sprint deliverables, not live today.
